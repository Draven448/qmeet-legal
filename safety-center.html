Dating Safety Guidelines

Getting to Know Someone

Do:

  • Take your time getting to know the person
  • Video chat before meeting in person
  • Ask questions and look for consistency
  • Trust your instincts (if it feels off, it is)

Don't:

  • Rush into meeting someone
  • Share personal info (address, workplace) too quickly
  • Ignore red flags or concerning behavior
  • Feel pressured to do anything uncomfortable

Planning Your First Date

1. Choose a Public Location

Coffee shops, restaurants, or busy parks are ideal. Avoid isolated or private locations for first meetings.

2. Tell Someone Your Plans

Share date details (location, person, time) with a friend or family member. Set up check-in times.

3. Arrange Your Own Transportation

Drive yourself or use a ride-sharing service. Don't accept rides from your date initially.

Support and Resources

If you're in immediate danger:

Call local emergency services (911, 999, 112, etc.) immediately.
